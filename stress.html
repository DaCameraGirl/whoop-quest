📖 How to read your WHOOP stress signals

Each metric tells you something different about how stressed / recovered your body is. Here's what to look for:

❤️ Recovery %

Your overall readiness score. Combines HRV, RHR, sleep, and strain into one number. This is the single best stress indicator.

67–100% Green 34–66% Yellow 0–33% Red

Stress signal: Sustained red/yellow = your body is struggling. A sharp drop (e.g. 96% → 28%) = acute stress event.

⚡ HRV (Heart Rate Variability)

Measures the variation in time between heartbeats (in milliseconds). Higher = your nervous system is balanced and recovering well.

40+ ms Good 20–40 ms OK <20 ms Low

Stress signal: HRV crashes under acute stress. A drop of 20+ ms overnight is a big red flag. Yours crashed from 61.2 → 15.1 ms after the roommate incident.

🫀 RHR (Resting Heart Rate)

Your heart rate while sleeping, in beats per minute. Lower = calmer, better recovered. Rises when your body is fighting stress, illness, or poor sleep.

60–70 bpm Good 71–78 bpm OK 79+ bpm Elevated

Stress signal: RHR spikes 5+ bpm above your baseline = stress / poor recovery. Yours spiked to 83 bpm the morning after the roommate incident (baseline ~74).

💤 Sleep Duration

Total time asleep. WHOOP tracks actual sleep, not just time in bed.

7–9h Good 5–7h OK <5h Low

Stress signal: Stress causes fragmented sleep, trouble falling asleep, early waking. Look for nights under 5h or big swings.

🎯 Sleep Performance

How much sleep you got vs. how much your body needed. 100% = you met your full sleep need.

70%+ Good 50–69% OK <50% Low

Stress signal: Low performance = sleep debt building. Under 50% repeatedly = cumulative stress.

🔔 Sleep Disturbances

Number of times you woke up during the night. More disturbances = more fragmented, less restorative sleep.

0–7 Low 8–12 Mid 13+ High

Stress signal: Anxiety and stress cause frequent waking. A jump in disturbances often precedes a recovery crash.

💪 Strain

Cardiovascular load for the day, 0–21 scale. Physical activity + life stress both contribute.

0–9 Light 10–13 Moderate 14+ High

Stress signal: High strain + low recovery = overreaching. Emotional stress also drives strain up even without exercise.

📊 Putting it together

Acute stress signature: Recovery crashes, HRV drops sharply, RHR spikes, sleep fragments (more disturbances), sleep performance drops.

Chronic stress signature: Recovery stuck yellow/red for days, HRV suppressed below baseline, RHR elevated, sleep debt accumulating.

Recovery signature: Recovery climbs back to green, HRV rebounds, RHR drops, sleep stabilizes and deepens.
